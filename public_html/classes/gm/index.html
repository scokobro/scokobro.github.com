    Globalisation and Media

    Internationalisation, Americanisation, globalisation and various aspects of mass media production and consumption.

    Reading List

    Syllabus

    Course Outline and Method

    This course offers students the opportunity to consider the various and complex relations between the ‘mass media’ and a variety of understandings of the term ‘globalisation’. After an initial period dealing with the theoretical background to media globalisation, sessions are grouped around four themes: media corporations, the knowledge economy, media cultures and public policy.

    Student Attainment Objectives

    An introductory knowledge of theories of media globalisation.

    • An appreciation of the complexity and variety of media/globalisation relationships.
    • Specific knowledge of the media system of a country chosen by the student

    Session Schedule

    1 Introduction
    Explaining the structure of the course, assessment, and expectations
    2 – 4 Introducing theories of global media
    From Critical Political Economy to Cultural Studies.
    5 – 6 Media Corporations
    Media multinationals in the global economy
    7 – 8 The Knowledge Economy
    Media imperialism, networks and production
    9 – 11 Media Cultures
    Lived culture and identity
    12 – 13 Public Policy
    National and international media policy regimes
    14 Essay Preparation / Tutorials
    Advice and guidance on writing, data gathering, presentation etc.
    15 Summary and Review
    Re-evaluating media globalisation.

    Assessment

    Attendance and Participation (40%):

    Students are expected to prepare for class by reading the set texts and by preparing questions arising to discuss in class.

    Final essay (60%):

    3000 words. Due at the end of week 15. This will be an argumentative essay which uses ideas and issues discussed during the course in regard to a national media system of the student’s choice. The title will be decided in discussion with the course tutor and there will be time set aside during week 14 for a final review before submission.

    Note

    This syllabus is a guide to likely course content, it is fairly flexible and whenever possible attempts will be made to adjust the course to reflect students’ particular interests.
